🌲 Forest Ecosystem

Explore the lush world of forests and their inhabitants

Forests are complex ecosystems that include trees, plants, animals, insects, fungi, and microorganisms. They maintain the balance of oxygen and carbon dioxide, provide shelter for wildlife, and support biodiversity. The dense canopy of trees allows sunlight to filter through, creating distinct layers in the forest, each with its unique organisms.

The forest food chain starts with producers such as trees and plants, followed by herbivores like deer and insects, carnivores like foxes and birds, and apex predators such as wolves. Decomposers, including fungi and bacteria, recycle nutrients back into the soil, completing the cycle.

Example Food Chain:
☀️ Sun → 🌱 Plants → 🐇 Rabbit → 🦊 Fox → 🐺 Wolf

Forest Facts

Trees cover approximately 31% of Earth's land area, providing habitat to over 80% of terrestrial species.

Importance

Forests purify air and water, prevent soil erosion, and provide raw materials like wood and medicine.
