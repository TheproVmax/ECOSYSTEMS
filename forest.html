Temperate Forest

Temperate forests are a terrestrial type of forest. The range of temperature in temperate forests is 25° - 50° (north to south). Its made up of four distinct seasons, from warm summers to cool winters. It can support a large variety of species due to its semi-tropical nature and its moderate to high precipitation.

Some wildlife that lives in temperate forests include red foxes, grey wolves, rabbits, berry bushes, and many more. Temperate forests also contain deciduous trees and coniferous trees. Temperate forests are mainly located in the Northern Hemisphere, existing in places like eastern North America, northeastern Asia, and many other countries and regions.

The health of a temperate forest is determined by many non-living factors such as oxygen, water, shade, rocks, and sun. They also have various interactions between biotic and abiotic components such as mammals drinking water from rivers, reptiles and amphibians taking shelter under rocks, and various plants and trees taking energy from the sun.
