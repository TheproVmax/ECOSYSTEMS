🌲 Forest

Forests are complex ecosystems that include trees, plants, animals, insects, fungi, and microorganisms. They maintain the balance of oxygen and carbon dioxide, provide shelter for wildlife, and support biodiversity.

The forest food chain starts with producers such as trees and plants, followed by herbivores like deer and insects, carnivores like foxes and birds, and apex predators such as wolves. Decomposers recycle nutrients back into the soil, completing the cycle.

Forests have layers: canopy, understory, shrub layer, and forest floor. Each layer hosts unique organisms, from birds in the canopy to insects and fungi on the forest floor.

Example Food Chain:
☀️ Sun → 🌱 Plants → 🐇 Rabbit → 🦊 Fox → 🐺 Wolf
