🏞️ Lake Ecosystem

Dive into the rich biodiversity of lakes

Main Description

A lake ecosystem is a community of living organisms (biotic factors) that interact with each other and their non-living surroundings (abiotic factors) in a still body of freshwater, such as a lake or pond.

Zones

These ecosystems are divided into zones based on depth and light penetration. The littoral zone near the shore is shallow and rich with life like plants, frogs, turtles, and insects. The limnetic zone in open water allows sunlight for algae growth, feeding small fish and other animals.

Food Web

The lake food web includes primary producers like algae, primary consumers such as zooplankton and small fish, secondary consumers like larger fish and birds, and decomposers like bacteria recycling nutrients.
