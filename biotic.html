A Brief Overview On Biotic and Abiotic Components

Biotic Components

Biotic components are components that are living things. You may have heard about the prefix "bio," which means life. Similarly, biotic components are living things that need energy, also known as food, to survive. A couple examples of biotic components are animals, humans like you and me, and pretty much anything that's living.


Abiotic Components

Abiotic components are the complete opposite of biotic components. Abiotic components are the non-living components in an ecosystem. Some examples of them could be rocks, the sun, water, and many more. While abiotic components are non-living, biotic components still need them in their daily lives. The reason is because abiotic components can help provide shelter, shade, perfect temperature, and many other factors.
